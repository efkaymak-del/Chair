Eastern Mediterranean University

The Reactive Trap

Currently, our administrative burden is concentrated, reactive, and deadline-driven.

  • Short-notice data demands from the Rector's office.
  • Accreditation evidence scrambled together just before site visits.
  • Committee and administrative workloads that are invisible, relying on the same few people.

The goal of this chairmanship: Give you back your time for teaching and research.

The Governing Philosophy

1. Data as a Byproduct. Administration shouldn't be a separate task. Data should be captured effortlessly at the moment the work happens.

2. Capture Once, Serve Many. A single database feeds Rectorate requests, STAR & FIBAA evidence (Kanıt Dosyası), and internal planning.

3. Good vs. Bad Friction. We eliminate the friction of data entry and filing. We fiercely protect the friction of Council Democracy—minutes are auto-drafted, but finalized only by your vote.

Portfolios & True Transparency

To avoid committee burnout, we move to a Portfolio Model. Each member owns a specific domain, with the Chair available as an optional working partner.

The Vice Chair leads the Reporting Spine, aggregating our data to interface with the Rectorate and feeding the DKGK.

Most importantly: We use EMU’s Sınıf Dışı Faaliyet Denklik Tüzüğü as our currency of fairness. Total load (Teaching + Out-of-class points) becomes fully transparent to the Council.

Reclaiming Our Time

A proactive department is not one that does more administrative work. It is one that designs systems so the administrative work does itself.

By capturing data smoothly, aligning with EMU regulations, and maintaining total transparency, we protect our programs from consolidation and protect our time for what matters.
