A Strategy for the Coming Term

Erol Kaymak · Candidate for Chair

The Stakes

Why Proactive, Why Now

Declining enrollment is the threat behind every other problem we face.

Falling numbers invite top-down consolidation. A department that looks reactive, opaque, and under-subscribed makes itself a candidate for it.

The answer is not to work harder inside the old reactive cycle. It is to become visibly proactive, efficient, and interdisciplinary — to make ourselves indispensable.

The Problem We Inherit

The Reactive Trap

  • Short-notice data demands from the Rector's office.
  • Accreditation evidence scrambled together just before site visits.
  • Committee and administrative loads that are invisible — and fall on the same few people.

This is not anyone's failing. It is a system that forces good colleagues into permanent firefighting. Our task is to redesign the system, not blame the firefighters.

Thesis

Record Once. Report Many.

We already report the same facts to four masters — STAR, the Rector's Faaliyet criteria, YÖK, and our own planning.

Capture that information once, at the moment the work happens, and a single pool serves all four. The drudgery disappears; the evidence accumulates by itself.

Governing Philosophy

Good Friction vs. Bad Friction

We eliminate

The friction of data entry, formatting, filing, and chasing information before a deadline.

We protect

The friction of Council democracy. Minutes may be auto-drafted, but become official only by our review and our vote.

I am not removing every step — only the steps that do not deserve our judgement.

Retention · Our Competitive Edge

Our Size Is Our Strength

We cannot out-scale the large quantitative departments. We can out-care them.

  • Knowing each student's name builds agency — and agency, in my experience, predicts outcomes.
  • Retention is won or lost in the gateway courses. We must be on top of attendance early, and follow up with care, not punishment.
  • To attract students to our electives, our courses must be at once less threatening and more enticing.

Pillar III · Interdisciplinary Horizon

An Invitation, With a Dividend

Declining numbers are best answered by sharing resources, not defending silos. Our IPE teaching already bridges politics, economics and international relations — a natural basis for a shared interdisciplinary programs with the Faculty of Business and Economics.

It is an invitation requiring the Dean's and other chairs' partnership. But it also pays a concrete dividend: curricular reform that adds tutorial hours helps us justify the Teaching Assistant assignments we currently lack — working smarter, not just harder.

Governance · What Is Settled

Committees Decide; Portfolios Feed

Some bodies are fixed by Council decision and stay as they are:

  • The accreditation committee (DKGK), which owns our STAR evidence file (Kanıt Dosyası).
  • The external Advisory Board (Danışma Kurulu) and the Öğrenim Güvence Komitesi.
  • The standing Graduate Affairs Committee for MA/PhD admissions.

New portfolios do not replace these. They are tributaries: each captures its domain's data, which flows up into the evidence file the committees curate.

Governance · For the Council to Decide

Open Questions — Not Decrees

1. Should the Vice Chair and the head of the DKGK be the same person?
Consolidation gives one clean reporting spine; separation gives quality assurance more independence. My leaning: consolidate — but this is the Council's call.
2. How do we distribute portfolios fairly?
Each member owns one; pairing with the Chair is voluntary; total load stays visible via the EMU Faaliyet metric.
3. Who carries our Faculty-facing roles — curriculum representative, Dean's-office liaison?
These need not all sit inside the department. We will assign them deliberately, together.

Our Brand

Transparency as Fairness

When every role and activity is recorded and visible, workload stops being a matter of impression and becomes a matter of fact.

Using EMU's Sınıf Dışı Faaliyet Denklik Tüzüğü as our shared currency, the Council can see — and balance — teaching, administrative and committee loads together. Some colleagues have long carried more than their share. A transparent system is how we put that right.

Live Demo

The Council's Own Record

While we have been talking, our discussion has been quietly transcribed.

A complete draft of today's minutes — and even draft Council decisions in STAR form — can be generated and filed in our shared drive in moments.

It awaits only one thing: the Council's review and democratic approval.

My Commitment

Leadership by Example

I will not ask of colleagues what I will not give first.

  • I will fill our instructor quotas as the first priority, then teach as many graduate courses — at discount where needed — as it takes to keep our programs alive.
  • I will help monitor attendance and join the effort to reach students who are slipping.
  • I will join students beyond the classroom — projects, trips, and the work that builds belonging.

I cannot do it all alone. But I can build the framework, and carry my share of the load.

Thank you.

I welcome your questions — and your judgement.
